This phase tests the local alignment condition directly. For each Phase~B grid cell we compute the exact one-step peer correction and project its probability-space displacement onto the direction from the initial policy to $(C,C)$. We then ask whether the \emph{magnitude} of this scalar predicts cells that fail under PG but succeed under Meta-MAPG.

A structural property of Stag Hunt (and coordination games generally) is that the peer correction always has non-negative projection toward the cooperative equilibrium: the cross-gradient term in a game with aligned payoffs cannot push \emph{away} from $(C,C)$. The test is therefore whether \emph{larger} peer projection predicts gain, not whether sign discriminates. Phase~V and~Z test sign discrimination across different payoff geometries.
